About Me

I'm a lifelong learner, constantly asking questions and trying to figure out the world around me. I was raised in Bangkok, Thailand, and had the great privilege of traveling and exploring the many cultures of Southeast Asia through competitive sports (basketball, football/soccer, volleyball, swimming). I moved to Portland, Oregon when I was 17, leaving behind my parents to live with my 90-yo grandmother and 19-yo brother to pursue a dream of playing basketball in college. After two socially and emotionally challenging highschool years (with lots of growth), I decided to go play Division 1 volleyball at a small school, Saint Francis University, in Loretto, Pennsylvania. I only stayed a year before transferring schools to Oregon State University, where I'm graduating with a B.S. in Computer Science and a minor in Business & Entrepreneurship.

Growing up in Bangkok, Thailand, I had the incredible opportunities to witness a culture where family, respect, and kindness are cornerstones of the culture. This taught me to expand my definition of family and want to help support even those outside of my immediate family. I see so much potential in each person but a lack of belief (whether self-inflicted or due to systemic oppression) that holds so many of us back. I place incredible importance on showing up daily BEing and growing into the person I want to be - loving, caring, real - so that I can inspire and empower those around me to be their best selves.
